### Abstract
Periodontal disease is a widespread condition that begins with a soft bacterial biofilm and can progress to systemic organ damage if left unmanaged.

### Pathological Progression
1. **Biofilm Formation:** Within hours of eating, salivary glycoproteins form a plaque film on the teeth, providing a breeding ground for aerobic bacteria.
2. **Calculus Mineralization:** If plaque isn't removed, minerals in the saliva harden it into calculus (tartar) within 48 to 72 hours.
3. **Subgingival Invasion:** Anaerobic bacteria migrate beneath the gumline, destroying the supportive periodontal ligament and alveolar bone. This leads to tooth loss and allows bacteria to enter the bloodstream, potentially damaging the heart, kidneys, and liver.

### Home Care Protocol
* **Mechanical Brushing:** Daily brushing with enzymatic pet toothpaste remains the gold standard for breaking up the plaque biofilm before it mineralizes.
* **Chemical Rinse:** Chlorhexidine gluconate solutions ($0.12\%$) bind to oral tissues, providing prolonged antibacterial activity to help manage severe gingivitis.
