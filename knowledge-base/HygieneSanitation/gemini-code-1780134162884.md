### Abstract
*Toxoplasma gondii* is a zoonotic protozoan parasite with cats serving as the definitive host. This article covers the parasite's life cycle and safely managing litterbox hygiene to prevent human infection.

### Parasitic Lifecycle Transmission
Cats typically contract *T. gondii* by consuming infected wild prey containing tissue cysts. The parasite reproduces inside the feline intestines, resulting in the shedding of millions of unsporulated oocysts in the cat's feces for a brief 1-to-2-week period.

### Safe Sanitation Practices
* **Daily Scooping:** Freshly shed oocysts require 1 to 5 days in the environment to undergo sporulation before they become infectious to humans. Cleaning the litterbox daily removes them before they reach this infectious stage.
* **High-Temperature Cleansing:** Wash the litter tray with boiling water ($>140^\circ\text{F}$ or $60^\circ\text{C}$) to mechanically kill sticky oocysts. Chemical disinfectants are largely ineffective against this specific oocyst wall.
* **Pregnancy Safety:** Pregnant or immunocompromised individuals should avoid handling cat litter entirely due to the risk of congenital toxoplasmosis. If unavoidable, wear disposable gloves and wash hands thoroughly with soap and warm water immediately afterward.
