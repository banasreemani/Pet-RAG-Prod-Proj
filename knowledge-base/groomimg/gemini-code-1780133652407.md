### Abstract
The canine and feline epidermal layer is only 3 to 5 cells thick, compared to the 10 to 15 layers found in human skin. This thin profile makes it highly vulnerable to chemical irritation and thermal damage. This article covers safe bathing practices.

### The pH Dynamic and Shampoo Selection
Human skin is acidic, maintaining an average pH of roughly 5.5. In contrast, the skin of domestic dogs and cats ranges from a neutral to alkaline pH of 6.2 to 7.5. Using human shampoos disrupts this delicate acid mantle, stripping away vital surface lipids and causing dry, itchy skin and secondary infections.

### Temperature Constraints
* **Ideal Range:** Water must be maintained at a lukewarm temperature between 95°F and 100°F (35°C–38°C).
* **Hyperthermia Risk:** Water temperatures above 103°F (39.4°C) can rapidly raise an animal's core body temperature, causing heat stress. This risk is especially high in brachycephalic (flat-faced) breeds, who have a limited capacity for panting-based cooling.
* **Hypothermia Prevention:** Thoroughly dry the animal with towels or a low-heat, high-velocity dryer to prevent shivering and a drop in core temperature, particularly in small or thin-coated breeds.
