### Abstract
Respiratory distress (dyspnea) is an immediate, life-threatening emergency. This document establishes clinical baselines for identifying respiratory distress and monitoring tissue oxygenation.

### Quantitative Identification Metrics
* **Normal Resting Respiratory Rate (RRR):** Healthy dogs and cats should take 15 to 30 breaths per minute while resting or sleeping.
* **Tachypnea Threshold:** A persistent resting respiratory rate above 40 breaths per minute warrants immediate veterinary assessment.

### Visual Presentation of Distress
* **Orthopneic Posture:** The animal stands or sits with its neck extended, elbows flared wide outward, and open-mouth panting (especially critical in cats) to minimize airway resistance.
* **Increased Effort:** Visible straining of the chest muscles, abdominal pumping, or deep indents between the ribs (intercostal retractions) during inhalation.
* **Cyanosis:** Mucous membranes (gums, tongue) turning blue or purple, signaling severe oxygen deprivation (hypoxia). This is an absolute emergency.
