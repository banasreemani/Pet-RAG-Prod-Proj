### Abstract
Feline urethral obstruction—a physical blockage of the urinary tract—is an acute emergency seen primarily in male cats due to their long, narrow anatomy.

### Behavioral Presentation
* **Stranguria:** The cat visits the litterbox repeatedly, straining long and hard while producing little to no urine. This behavior is frequently mistaken for constipation.
* **Vocalization:** The cat cries out, howls, or yowls in pain while straining in the box or grooming its perineal region.
* **Periuria:** Attempting to urinate outside the box on cool surfaces like tile, bathtubs, or sinks.

### Pathological Progression
If the blockage persists beyond 24 hours, the kidneys can no longer filter waste products, leading to post-renal azotemia. Potassium builds up in the bloodstream (hyperkalemia), which can trigger fatal cardiac arrhythmias and sudden heart failure within 48 to 72 hours.
