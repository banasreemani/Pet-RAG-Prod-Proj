### Abstract
Checking the color of the mucous membranes and measuring Capillary Refill Time (CRT) provides a quick, reliable assessment of an animal's cardiovascular circulation and tissue perfusion.

### Diagnostic Testing Protocol
Gently lift the upper lip to expose the gum tissue. Press firmly with a finger to blanch the tissue white, then release and count the seconds it takes for the pink color to return.

### Clinical Metrics

| CRT / Membrane Presentation | Pathological Interpretation | Indicated Emergency State |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **< 1 Second** | Hyperdynamic state; rapid, compensatory blood flow. | Early endotoxic shock, systemic inflammation, or hyperthermia. |
| **1–2 Seconds** | Normal cardiovascular function. | Stable, healthy baseline perfusion. |
| **> 2.5 Seconds** | Poor peripheral circulation; vasoconstriction. | Dehydration, hypovolemic shock, or congestive heart failure. |
| **Pale / White Gums** | Severe lack of red blood cells or poor blood flow. | Severe internal hemorrhage, acute anemia, or advanced shock. |
| **Muddy / Gray Gums** | Poor oxygenation combined with toxemia. | End-stage septic shock or systemic organ failure. |
