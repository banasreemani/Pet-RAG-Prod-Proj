### Abstract
Feline Idiopathic Cystitis (FIC) is an inflammatory bladder condition closely linked to environmental stress. It is a primary cause of litterbox aversion and inappropriate urination (periuria).

### The Stress-Bladder Connection
Stress activates a cat's sympathetic nervous system, altering the protective glycosaminoglycan (GAG) layer that lines the bladder wall. This allows harsh urine to irritate the underlying tissue, causing pain and burning that the cat quickly associates with the litterbox itself.

### Environmental Management Checklist
* **The N+1 Rule:** Always provide one more litterbox than the total number of cats in the household (e.g., a 3-cat household requires at least 4 distinct litterboxes).
* **Box Location:** Place boxes in quiet, low-traffic areas with multiple clear exit paths to prevent cats from feeling trapped by other pets.
* **Substrate Preference:** Most domestic cats prefer fine-grained, unscented, clumping clay substrates. Avoid harsh chemical scents or liners that can irritate sensitive paws.
