### Abstract
While post-vaccinal adverse reactions are statistically rare, they require rapid identification and clinical triage to prevent life-threatening outcomes.

### Severity Levels of Adverse Events
1. **Mild (Mild Immune Response):** Low-grade fever, mild lethargy, and temporary localized swelling at the injection site. These signs typically resolve on their own within 24 to 48 hours.
2. **Moderate (Type I Hypersensitivity):** Development of urticaria (hives), facial angioedema (swelling of the muzzle and eyelids), and intense pruritus (itching).
3. **Severe (Anaphylactic Shock):** Sudden collapse, systemic hypotension, dyspnea (breathing difficulty), pale mucous membranes, and acute vomiting or diarrhea. This is an immediate, life-threatening emergency.

### Emergency Triage Interventions
If anaphylaxis occurs, immediate veterinary treatment is critical. The primary treatment is the prompt intravenous or intramuscular administration of epinephrine ($1:10000$ solution dilution adjusted by body mass). This is typically paired with rapid intravenous fluid therapy to stabilize blood pressure, along with corticosteroids and antihistamines.
