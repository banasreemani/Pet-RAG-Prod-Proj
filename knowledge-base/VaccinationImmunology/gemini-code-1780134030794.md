### Abstract
Serum antibody titer testing measures circulating antibodies to determine if an animal maintains protective immunity against specific diseases, offering an alternative to automatic rotational boosters.

### Science of Titer Evaluation
Using techniques like Enzyme-Linked Immunosorbent Assay (ELISA) or Hemagglutination Inhibition (HI), titers measure the concentration of specific IgG antibodies in a blood sample. Positive titers for Canine Parvovirus, Canine Distemper, and Feline Panleukopenia show a strong correlation with protection against active infection.

### Clinical Trade-offs

| Factor | Automatic Rotational Boosters | Serum Titer Testing |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Pros** | Low cost, ensures compliance with local laws, straightforward protocol. | Avoids unnecessary injections; minimizes risks for animals with a history of vaccine reactions. |
| **Cons** | Can cause minor over-vaccination of already protected animals. | Higher cost; does not measure cell-mediated immunity or guarantee protection against mucosal pathogens like *Bordetella*. |
