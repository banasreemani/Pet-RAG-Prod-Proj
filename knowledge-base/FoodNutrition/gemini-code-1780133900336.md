### Abstract
Nutritional needs shift dramatically across an animal's lifespan. This article covers the macronutrient changes required between growth, adult maintenance, and senior life stages.

### Growth Profiles (Puppies & Kittens)
Growing animals require high energy density and precise mineral ratios to support skeletal and muscular development.
* **Macronutrient Targets:** Diets should provide a minimum of 22% dry matter (DM) protein for puppies and 30% DM protein for kittens.
* **Calcium-to-Phosphorus Ratio:** For large-breed puppies, this ratio must be tightly managed between 1.1:1 and 1.3:1. Deviations can lead to developmental orthopedic disorders like hip dysplasia or osteochondrosis dissecans.

### Adult Maintenance
* **Strategic Focus:** Maintaining ideal lean body mass and preventing obesity. Total daily energy requirements (DER) should be adjusted based on the animal's body condition score (BCS).

### Senior Profiles
* **Strategic Focus:** Managing metabolic decline and protecting organ function. Senior diets typically require a reduction in overall sodium and phosphorus to ease the workload on aging kidneys, alongside an increase in highly digestible proteins to combat age-related muscle wasting (sarcopenia).
