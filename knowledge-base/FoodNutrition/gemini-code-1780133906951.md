### Abstract
This article evaluates the processing differences and microbiological safety profiles of raw meat diets compared to standard thermal extrusion (kibble).

### Thermal Extrusion Processing
Standard commercial kibble is produced using an extrusion process that subjects ingredients to temperatures above 250°F (121°C) and high pressure. This environment reliably neutralizes vegetative bacterial pathogens, ensuring a shelf-stable product.

### Microbiological Hazards of Raw Feeding
Raw meat diets bypass these critical pathogen-reduction steps. Peer-reviewed epidemiological tracking consistently reveals a high prevalence of *Salmonella enterica*, *Listeria monocytogenes*, and *Campylobacter* species in raw pet food products.

### Public Health and Zoonotic Implications
* **Shedding Risk:** Animals fed raw diets can asymptomatically shed viable pathogens in their feces and saliva, presenting a direct infection risk to human family members.
* **Immune Vulnerability:** Households with young children, elderly individuals, or immunocompromised residents should strictly avoid raw meat pet diets due to the heightened risk of severe zoonotic foodborne illness.
