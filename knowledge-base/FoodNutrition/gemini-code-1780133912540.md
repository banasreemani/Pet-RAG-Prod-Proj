### Abstract
Gastric Dilatation-Volvulus (GDV) is a life-threatening, acute condition where the stomach distends with gas and rotates along its longitudinal axis. This article details dietary adjustments that help lower the risk of this emergency.

### Epidemiological Risk Factors
Large, deep-chested canine breeds (such as Great Danes, Standard Poodles, and German Shepherds) are anatomically predisposed to GDV.

### Evidence-Based Prevention Strategies
* **Meal Frequency:** Divide the total daily food allocation into 2 or 3 smaller meals. Feeding a single, large meal stretches the gastric ligaments over time, increasing the risk of torsion.
* **Kibble Geometry:** Avoid formulas made entirely of very small kibble pieces that lead to rapid gulping. Opt for larger kibbles that encourage chewing, or use slow-feeder bowls.
* **Ingredient Layout:** Diets listing fat or oil within the first four ingredients have been correlated with delayed gastric emptying, which can increase gas production.
* **Exercise Windows:** Strictly restrict vigorous physical activity, running, and heavy water consumption for 1 hour before and 2 hours after meals to prevent the heavy stomach from shifting or rotating.
