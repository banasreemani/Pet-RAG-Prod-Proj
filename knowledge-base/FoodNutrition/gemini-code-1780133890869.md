### Abstract
Cats are taxonomically obligate carnivores. Their metabolic pathways are hardwired to process animal tissues, leaving them unable to safely adapt to vegetarian or vegan diets.

### Key Metabolic Profiles
* **Constant Gluconeogenesis:** Cats cannot downregulate their hepatic amino acid catabolizing enzymes. They continually break down proteins for energy, even when dietary intake is low.
* **Essential Taurine Requirements:** Unlike dogs, cats cannot synthesize taurine from precursor amino acids like cysteine or methionine. They must absorb taurine directly from animal proteins.

### Pathological Consequences of Taurine Deficiency
1. **Dilated Cardiomyopathy (DCM):** A lack of taurine compromises myocardial cell contractility, causing the heart chambers to thin and stretch, ultimately leading to congestive heart failure.
2. **Central Retinal Degeneration (FCRD):** Taurine is vital for maintaining high concentrations in the photoreceptor cells of the retina. Deficiency leads to permanent, irreversible blindness.
