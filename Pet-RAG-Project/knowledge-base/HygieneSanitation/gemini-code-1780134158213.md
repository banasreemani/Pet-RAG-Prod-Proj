### Abstract
Non-enveloped viruses, such as Canine Parvovirus (CPV-2) and Feline Panleukopenia (FPV), are highly stable and resistant to many standard household cleaners. This article breaks down effective chemical disinfection protocols.

### Disinfectant Chemistry Comparison

| Chemical Class | Example Agents | Mechanism | Parvoviral Efficacy | Required Contact Time |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Quaternary Ammonium** | Standard benzalkonium formulas | Disrupts lipophilic cell membranes. | **Ineffective** against non-enveloped viral proteins. | N/A |
| **Halogens** | Sodium Hypochlorite (Bleach at 1:32 dilution) | Causes rapid protein oxidation and denatures viral DNA/RNA. | **Highly Effective** on pre-cleaned surfaces. | 10 Minutes (Wet) |
| **Peroxygen Compounds** | Potassium Peroxymonosulfate / Accelerated Hydrogen Peroxide | Oxidizes cell walls and disrupts structural protein bonds. | **Highly Effective** with excellent biofilm penetration. | 5–10 Minutes |

### Execution Rules
Always clean away organic matter (feces, vomit, dirt) before applying disinfectants. Organic debris neutralizes chlorine compounds, rendering bleach washes ineffective.
