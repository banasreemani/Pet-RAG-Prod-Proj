### Abstract
Managing a flea (*Ctenocephalides felis*) infestation requires a dual approach that targets both the active adults on the pet and the developing life stages embedded in the surrounding environment.

### The Flea Life Cycle Pyramid
Adult fleas living on the pet represent only about 5% of the total infestation. The remaining 95% exists in the environment as eggs (50%), larvae (35%), and pupae (10%).

### Comprehensive Eradication Strategy
* **On-Pet Treatment:** Administer adulticides (such as Isoxazoline compounds like fluralaner or afoxolaner) to quickly kill adult fleas and halt egg production.
* **Environmental Control (Insect Growth Regulators):** Use environmental sprays containing Insect Growth Regulators (IGRs) like lufenuron or pyriproxyfen. These mimic juvenile hormones, preventing flea larvae from pupating or developing into adults.
* **The Pupal Window Challenge:** Flea pupae spin sticky, protective silken cocoons that resist chemical sprays and vacuuming. Regular vacuuming is essential because the mechanical vibrations help stimulate pupae to emerge, exposing them to the active adulticides on treated pets.
