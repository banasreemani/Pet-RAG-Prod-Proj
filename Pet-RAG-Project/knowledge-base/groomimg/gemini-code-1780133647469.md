### Abstract
Feline skin is structurally thinner and less elastic than canine skin, making it far more vulnerable to tears and abrasions during grooming. This article outlines the precautions required for handling feline coats and minimizing trichobezoar (hairball) formation.

### Feline Coat Characteristics
Feline grooming serves essential thermoregulatory and behavioral functions. Over-grooming or systemic health issues (e.g., osteoarthritis) can result in dense matting along the dorsum, perineum, and caudal thighs.

### Safe Intervention Guidelines
* **Brushing Constraints:** Avoid harsh wire slicker brushes on thin-skinned or geriatric cats. Soft-bristle brushes or wide-toothed metal combs with rounded tips are preferred.
* **Undercoat Stripping Risks:** Fur-stripping tools with micro-blades must be used with minimal downward pressure. Repeated passes over the same anatomical zone can slice the epidermis, causing acute dermatitis.
* **Sedation Thresholds:** If a cat exhibits extreme stress or widespread matting, manual dematting should be abandoned in favor of low-stress chemical restraint (sedation) under veterinary supervision.
