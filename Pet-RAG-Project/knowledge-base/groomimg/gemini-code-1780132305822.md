### Abstract
Coat matting represents a dense compaction of shed undercoat hair intertwining with live guard hairs. If left unmanaged, matting progresses to "pelting," which restricts air flow to the dermis, traps moisture, and creates a microenvironment highly susceptible to bacterial dermatitis and localized skin necrosis.

### Technical Protocols
* **Shedding Cycle Intervention:** Double-coated breeds require regular extraction of the dead undercoat using an undercoat rake. This prevents the barbed structure of shed hair from locking around viable shafts.
* **Mechanical Dematting Limits:** Attempt mechanical detangling only on Grade 1 (loose) or Grade 2 (localized) mats. Use a high-slip silicone spray to reduce friction and isolate the mat. Work inward from the distal end of the hair shaft toward the follicle using a targeted slicker brush or a bladed dematting tool.
* **Pelting Intervention:** Grade 3 matting (extensive pelting close to the epidermis) must not be mechanically brushed out due to the high risk of severe traction alopecia and skin tears. The pelt must be clipped using a professional grooming clipper with a #10 or #30 blade safety guard, keeping the blade flat against the skin surface to avoid slicing tented skin.
