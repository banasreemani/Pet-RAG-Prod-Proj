### Abstract
Non-core vaccines are optional immunizations chosen based on an animal's geographic location, lifestyle, and individual risk of exposure.

### Canine Non-Core Evaluation
* **Leptospirosis:** Highly recommended for dogs with access to standing water, rivers, or areas frequented by wildlife. Protects against bacterial shedding in wild animal urine; this is a critical zoonotic pathogen that can cause acute kidney failure in humans.
* **Bordetella Bronchiseptica & Canine Influenza (H3N8/H3N2):** Essential for dogs that regularly visit high-density environments like boarding kennels, dog parks, or grooming facilities.
* **Borrelia Burgdorferi (Lyme Disease):** Targeted at dogs living in or traveling to tick-endemic regions with high populations of *Ixodes* ticks.

### Feline Non-Core Evaluation
* **Feline Leukemia Virus (FeLV):** Classed as non-core for mature adults, but strongly recommended by the AAFP for all kittens due to their high susceptibility. It remains essential for adult cats with outdoor access or those living in multi-cat environments with unknown FeLV status.
