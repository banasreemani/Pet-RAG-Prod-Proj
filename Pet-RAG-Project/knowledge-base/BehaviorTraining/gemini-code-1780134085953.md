### Abstract
Separation anxiety is a clinical condition driven by panic and an overactive distress response when an animal is isolated from its primary attachment figures. This article maps out systematic desensitization protocols.

### Clinical Presentation
Common signs include destructive behaviors focused around exit points (doors and windows), continuous vocalization, hypersalivation, and self-trauma.

### Systematic Behavior Modification Protocol
* **Isolating Pre-Departure Cues:** Identify the specific actions that trigger the animal's anxiety before you leave (such as picking up keys, putting on a coat, or moving toward the door). Perform these actions repeatedly throughout the day without actually leaving, breaking the link between the cue and real isolation.
* **Graduated Departures:** Establish step-by-step departures that stay below the animal's panic threshold. Begin with short absences lasting only seconds (e.g., stepping behind a closed interior door) and slowly increase the duration over several weeks.
* **Pharmacological Support:** In severe cases, behavior modification should be paired with veterinary-prescribed anxiolytics (like fluoxetine or clomipramine) to lower baseline cortisol levels and allow learning to occur.
