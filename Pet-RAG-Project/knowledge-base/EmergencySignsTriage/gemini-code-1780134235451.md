### Abstract
Canine Gastric Dilatation-Volvulus (GDV) progresses rapidly and can be fatal within hours. Recognizing early physical signs is vital for saving the animal's life.

### Primary Signs of Torsion
* **Unproductive Emesis:** The dog retches repeatedly, attempting to vomit every few minutes, but produces only foam or strings of thick saliva. This happens because the twisted stomach completely seals the entrance and exit.
* **Abdominal Tympany:** The stomach rapidly expands with trapped gas, making the abdomen look tight and distended. Tapping lightly behind the ribs produces a hollow, drum-like (tympanic) sound.
* **Rapid Systemic Decline:** As the expanding stomach presses on major blood vessels, it restricts blood return to the heart, leading to a weak pulse, rapid heart rate, pale gums, and sudden collapse.

### Emergency Response
GDV cannot be treated at home. If you suspect torsion, transport the dog to a surgical veterinary facility immediately; emergency decompression and surgery are required to untwist the stomach.
