### Abstract
Heatstroke occurs when an animal's core body temperature rises dangerously above its normal physiological range, risking widespread organ failure and internal complications.

### Diagnostic Thresholds
* **Normal Temperature Range:** 101.0°F to 102.5°F (38.3°C–39.2°C).
* **Critical Hyperthermia Level:** A core rectal temperature reaching or exceeding 105.0°F (40.5°C) indicates acute heatstroke, endangering cellular proteins and risking thermal damage to the brain and organs.

### Safe Field Cooling Protocol
1. **Move to Safety:** Immediately relocate the animal out of direct sunlight into an air-conditioned room or shaded area.
2. **Apply Cool Water:** Wet the fur down to the skin using lukewarm or cool tap water. Focus on high-circulation areas like the groin, armpits, and neck.
3. **Enhance Airflow:** Place a fan near the wet animal to maximize cooling through evaporation.
4. **Avoid Ice Water:** Do not use ice, ice baths, or freezing water. Extreme cold constricts blood vessels in the skin, trapping heat deep inside the body core and causing shivering, which generates more internal heat.
5. **Stop Point:** Cease active cooling measures once the rectal temperature drops to 103.5°F (39.7°C) to prevent overcorrecting into dangerous hypothermia. Transport the animal to a clinic immediately to check for internal bleeding disorders (DIC).
